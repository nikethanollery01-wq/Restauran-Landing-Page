To ensure your website's UI looks professional against that deep, dark charred wood background, you want a palette that offers high contrast for readability while maintaining a sophisticated "gastropub" aesthetic.

Since your background is a very dark, textured black/charcoal, here are the recommended hex codes:

### 1. Typography & Text Colors
You want to avoid pure white ($#FFFFFF$), as it can be too jarring against a dark background. A "warm white" or "cream" feels more organic and high-end.

* **Primary Heading:** `#F5F5F0` (Off-white/Cream)
* **Body Text:** `#D1D1CB` (Light Gray with a hint of warmth)
* **Accent Text (Labels/Small Caps):** `#C5A059` (Muted Gold - pulls from the roasted chicken skin)

---

### 2. Navigation Bar
To keep the "Hero" image the star of the show, the nav bar should be semi-transparent or a solid deep charcoal.

* **Nav Background:** `#121212` (Deep Charcoal)
* **Nav Text:** `#F5F5F0`
* **Nav Link (Active/Hover):** `#C5A059` (Muted Gold)

---

### 3. Action Buttons (CTA)
For the buttons, you want to pull colors from the food itself (the lemons and the golden-brown chicken) to create a "delicious" visual harmony.

| State | Hex Code | Visual Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Button** | `#D4AF37` | **Metallic Gold** (Pops against the black wood) |
| **Button Text** | `#1A1A1A` | **Dark Charcoal** (For maximum legibility on the gold) |
| **Hover State** | `#E6C35C` | **Bright Lemon Gold** (A lighter, "glowing" version of the button) |

---

### 4. Implementation Tip
Since your image has the subject on the **extreme right**, ensure your text container on the left has a subtle "Glow" or a very soft gradient overlay if the wood grain is too busy. 

**Quick CSS Tip for the Background:**
```css
/* To match the wood texture precisely */
body {
  background-color: #0A0A0A; 
  color: #F5F5F0;
}
```
